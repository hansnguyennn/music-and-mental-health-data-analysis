# music-and-mental-health-data-analysis
Analyzes how music relates to mental health outcomes, exploring whether its benefits differ based on individuals’ baseline anxiety and depression levels.

# Research Questions
1. Do people who report benefits from music start with higher anxiety and depression?
2. Is the effect of music on mental health statistically significant?
3. Are certain music genres associated with different mental health outcomes?

# Key Findings
People who say music improves their mental health tend to have higher baseline anxiety and depression. The relationship is statistically significant, suggesting the effect is not random. This indicates music may be especially beneficial for those starting from a worse mental health state.
